# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP): Internal Audit Process

This SOP defines the standardized workflow for Internal Audit activities within the Audit Management System.

---

## 1. Risk Assessment & Identification
**Objective**: To identify, analyze, and prioritize organizational risks.

1.  **Risk Identification**: Auditors must record potential risks in the **Risk Register**, categorized by domain (e.g., Financial, Operational, Technology, Compliance).
2.  **Risk Scoring**: Each risk is evaluated based on:
    - **Likelihood (1-5)**: Probability of the risk event occurring.
    - **Impact (1-5)**: Severity of the consequences if the risk materializes.
3.  **Heat Map Analysis**: The system automatically calculates the **Risk Score** (Likelihood × Impact). Risks with a score ≥ 15 are classified as **Critical** and prioritized for immediate audit coverage.

---

## 2. Strategic Audit Planning (3-Year Cycle)
**Objective**: To ensure comprehensive coverage of the Audit Universe over a multi-year period.

1.  **Define Auditable Entities**: Specify the departments, processes, or systems that comprise the "Audit Universe".
2.  **Resource Allocation**: Based on the Risk Assessment scores, entities are mapped to a 3-year strategic cycle:
    - **Year 1**: Critical and High-risk entities.
    - **Year 2**: Moderate-risk entities.
    - **Year 3**: Low-risk or newly established entities.
3.  **Plan Approval**: The Strategic Plan must be submitted and approved by the Audit Committee before proceeding to annual scheduling.

---

## 3. Annual Audit Planning
**Objective**: To schedule and staff specific audit engagements for the current fiscal year.

1.  **Extract Year 1 Entities**: Automatically pull all entities scheduled for "Year 1" from the Strategic Plan.
2.  **Create Engagements**: For each entity, create a formal **Audit Engagement**, defining its scope and objectives.
3.  **Assignment**: Assign a **Lead Auditor** and define start/end dates.
4.  **Quarterly Balancing**: Ensure audit load is balanced across the four quarters (Q1-Q4) to prevent auditor burnout.

---

## 4. Audit Execution & Reporting
**Objective**: To conduct fieldwork, document findings, and report results.

1.  **Opening Meeting**: Conduct a meeting with the auditee to discuss scope and timelines. Use the **Meetings** module to record attendance and minutes.
2.  **Fieldwork**: Document audit tests, evidence, and preliminary findings.
3.  **Reporting**: Generate an Audit Report with observations, risk implications, and management action plans.
4.  **Follow-up**: Track the implementation of agreed-upon recommendations.

---

> [!IMPORTANT]
> All audit activities must adhere to the International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing (IPPF).
